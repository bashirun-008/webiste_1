# Chapter 4: The Career Crossroad

Two days after the family dinner, the delicate balance of Meera's life was completely upended. She received a formal email from her company's corporate headquarters in London. She had been awarded her dream promotion—a vice-presidency role within the international infrastructure fund. The role was everything she had worked toward for six grueling years. The caveat was immediate and absolute: it required a mandatory, three-year relocation to Mumbai.

She sat in her office cubicle, the glowing text of the offer letter reflecting off her face. A deep, cold anxiety settled in her stomach. She knew the societal math all too well. In the traditional narrative, a woman's relocation for her husband's career was an expected sacrifice; a man relocating for his wife's career was a radical anomaly. Pursuing her ambition meant risking the fragile, beautiful connection she had just built with Ashwin.

She met Ashwin that evening on the concrete benches of the Besant Nagar beach. The sea was dark, the waves crashing violently against the shore as the salt air whipped around them. She handed him her phone, letting him read the text of the email in silence.

"It's an incredible opportunity, Meera," Ashwin said quietly, looking out at the dark horizon after a long pause. His voice held no anger, only a profound, reflective weight.

"If I take it, Ashwin, the families will use it as an excuse to break this match," Meera said, her eyes filling with tears. "They'll say it's proof that I'm not committed to a family life. I don't want to choose between my future and you."

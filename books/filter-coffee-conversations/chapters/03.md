# Chapter 3: The Family Dinner

The initial warmth of their private conversations met a substantial hurdle three weeks later during a formal dinner party at an upscale restaurant in Alwarpet. Both sets of parents sat around a long teakwood table, their smiles polite but their eyes scanning each other with the sharp, evaluative precision of veteran diplomats negotiating a trade treaty.

The tension began when the conversation shifted toward the future logistics of their careers. Ashwin's mother, an elegant woman wearing a traditional silk saree, adjusted her gold glass bangles and looked directly at Meera. "We are very proud of your professional achievements, Meera. But once the wedding is finalized, our family expects certain adjustments. Ashwin's firm is rooted here in Chennai, and managing a household requires a steady presence."

Meera felt her posture stiffen, the warm comfort of the relationship suddenly feeling threatened by the cold weight of ancient expectations. She looked at her own mother, who was giving her a desperate, pleading look from across the table, silently begging her to remain quiet, to nod, to play the expected role.

Before Meera could speak, Ashwin intervened, his voice calm but unyielding. "Amma, marriage isn't a relocation package for Meera. Her career isn't an ornament. Whatever choices we make about our home or our careers will be made as equals, based on our individual timelines, not an old template." The silence that followed was heavy, broken only by the polite, distant clinking of silverware from neighboring tables.

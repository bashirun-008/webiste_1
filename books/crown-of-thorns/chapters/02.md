# Chapter Two: The Second Life

Noor opened her eyes in a small, sparse room in the servants' quarter of the very same palace, seventeen years old, a court singer of minor rank with a voice that had, in this timeline she hadn't yet lived, not yet been discovered by anyone who mattered.

She understood, within minutes, with the particular vertigo of a woman who had once commanded an empire waking up as nobody at all, that she had been given a second life — years before her rise to the emperor's favor, years before the coup that would one day, in some other unraveled thread of time, take her life at the height of a wedding celebration.

She had, she calculated grimly, staring at hands that had not yet learned the calluses of holding real power, roughly a decade before the events that would eventually kill her. A decade to do what she had once done — rise, survive, rule in every way but name — except this time, armed with the exact names of every noble who would one day conspire against her, the exact date, seventeen years from now, that Fazil the servant boy would be turned against her, and the exact, precious knowledge of one man's unwavering loyalty, which she intended, this time, to properly deserve before it was ever tested again.

She began, that very week, singing in the outer courtyards where minor court officials gathered, patient in a way her past-life self, thrust suddenly and dramatically into favor, had never needed to be. She was not in a hurry to be discovered by the emperor. She was, this time, in a hurry to be discovered by exactly one person first.

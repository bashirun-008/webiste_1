# Chapter Five: The Emperor's Favor

By her nineteenth year in this second life, Noor's voice — and, increasingly, her carefully cultivated intelligence network of stewards and clerks who trusted her — had drawn the attention of Emperor Jahandar himself, an aging ruler whose interest in her was, this time, something Noor navigated with far more calculation than she had the first time she'd stumbled, dazzled and unprepared, into his favor.

She became, over the following two years, exactly what she had once been: the emperor's most trusted confidante, a woman whose counsel he sought on matters far beyond the harem's traditional boundaries, a power in the court that no title could fully capture and that made her, inevitably, a target for the same coalition of nobles who would one day, in the life she remembered, order her death.

The crucial difference, this time, was that she saw them coming years in advance. She knew which noble family would first propose the alliance against her — the Zahedi clan, resentful since a land dispute the emperor had ruled against them a decade earlier. She knew which minor official, a treasury clerk named Baqir, would eventually be bribed to falsify records implicating her in corruption as cover for the coup. She spent her years of favor quietly, patiently dismantling the coalition's foundations before it could ever fully form — exposing the Zahedi clan's own corruption before they could weaponize accusations against her, promoting Baqir's honest rival to the treasury post that would have let him falsify records in the first place, and, most carefully of all, ensuring that Fazil, the servant boy who would one day carry her poisoned wine, was never placed in her personal service at all, transferred instead, with a generous recommendation she made sure came from someone else entirely, to a post in a distant province where he would never be positioned to be turned against her.

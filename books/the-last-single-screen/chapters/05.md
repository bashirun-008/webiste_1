# Chapter 5: The Curtain Falls

By midnight, the crowd had reluctantly dispersed into the cool night air, leaving behind a floor littered with empty paper cups and the lingering energy of their laughter. Imtiaz walked through the empty rows of seats, his footsteps echoing loudly against the hollow walls. He reached the front row, looking up at the blank white screen that had defined every single day of his life.

Kabir walked down the center aisle, stopping right behind his father. The arrogance was gone from his face, replaced by a quiet, subdued reverence. He looked at the old pillars, understanding for the first time that his father wasn't fighting for a piece of property—he was protecting a sanctuary.

"They won't build a shopping mall here, Papa," Kabir said softly, his voice cutting through the stillness. "I spoke to the regional architect this evening while the movie was playing. We can apply for a heritage conservation status for the facade and the main structure. We can convert the lobby into a cultural archive and use the hall for classic film screenings and community theater. We don't have to erase it."

Imtiaz turned to look at his son, the moonlight reflecting off his white hair. He reached into his pocket and pulled out the old brass key to the main gates, placing it gently into Kabir's hand. The metal was cold, but Kabir's grip was firm.

Upstairs, Santosh turned off the cooling switches one by one. The hum of the machine slowly faded into complete silence. Imtiaz looked around the dark hall one last time, feeling no sadness, only a deep, profound gratitude. The age of the single screen had come to an end, but the light it had cast into the hearts of the city would never truly go dark.

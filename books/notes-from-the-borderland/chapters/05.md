# Chapter 5: The Audition Tape

The power returned on Thursday afternoon, bringing with it a frantic scramble to finish the project. Arenla took her phone, a heavy tripod borrowed from Toshi, and walked up to the flat, open terrace of their traditional house. The sky had cleared to a brilliant, crystalline blue, exposing the jagged, deep green ridges of the outer Himalayas stretching into the horizon.

She didn't wear the imported black hoodie this time. Instead, she had carefully draped her grandmother's indigo-dyed clan shawl over her shoulders, modifying it into an asymmetrical, modern streetwear design held together by a thick tactical belt. It was bold, unusual, and intensely personal.

Toshi hit the record button on the camera app. Arenla stood in the center of the frame, the mountain wind catching the edges of the blue-grey fabric.

The music started. She didn't just dance the rigid, factory-preset steps of the global pop routine. She infused it with the fluid, expansive gestures of the traditional folk dances she had watched her elders perform during the winter festivals. Her movements were large, powerful, and rooted, her sneakers clicking against the concrete floor like the ancient shuttle of a handloom.

When the music ended, she walked up to the lens, looked directly into the camera, and introduced herself not as a nameless applicant from a distant borderland, but as a daughter of Mokokchung, carrying her ancestors' geometry on her back.

That evening, sitting by her grandmother's bedside as the file upload indicator finally reached one hundred percent, Arenla didn't feel the old, desperate anxiety to escape. She watched the little green checkmark appear on her screen, knowing that wherever that digital video traveled—whether it landed on a desk in Seoul, Tokyo, or Mumbai—it carried the unyielding weight, smoke, and indigo of the hills she called home.

Chapter 2: The Café Protocol

They bypassed the formal family-arranged meeting structures, opting instead to meet at Rayar's Mess, a legendary, no-nonsense traditional eatery tucked away in a narrow alley near the Kapaleeshwarar Temple. The venue was a deliberate choice by Ashwin, an implicit test to see if she could appreciate the chaotic, authentic heart of the city over the air-conditioned isolation of a suburban luxury hotel.

Sitting across from each other on simple steel chairs, surrounded by the loud clatter of metal tumblers and the rich aroma of roasting coffee beans, the initial awkwardness quickly evaporated. Ashwin was a landscape architect, his hands slightly calloused and his eyes bright with a quiet, unhurried curiosity. Instead of asking her typical questions about her family lineage, dietary preferences, or horoscopes, he leaned forward over the laminate table.

"Tell me about the worst apartment you ever rented in Bangalore," Ashwin said, a genuine smile breaking across his face as the waiter dropped two stainless steel tumblers of frothy, dark filter coffee between them.

Meera laughed, taking a sip of the scalding liquid. The coffee was perfect—bitter, chicory-rich, and smooth. Over the next two hours, their conversation flowed seamlessly, moving from shared complaints about urban traffic to their deeper, unspoken anxieties about preserving their individuality within the heavy, structured mechanism of a traditional Indian marriage. For the first time in years, Meera felt like she was being seen, not as an eligible portfolio, but as herself.

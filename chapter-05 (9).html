Chapter 1: The Profile Match

The yellow evening light of Mylapore always felt warm, catching the dust over the brass lamps inside Meera's family home. At twenty-eight, Meera was perfectly content with her life as a senior financial analyst, navigating market valuations by day and reading fiction by night. Her mother, however, viewed her single status as a minor domestic crisis that required urgent intervention. Under extreme maternal pressure, Meera sat cross-legged on her bed, staring down at a newly created profile on a premium matrimonial application.

The app was a modern digital marketplace of expectations. She swiped past endless profiles containing meticulously curated corporate resumes, property declarations, and generic statements about "balancing traditional values with a modern outlook." It all felt transactional, like auditing a balance sheet rather than discovering a human being. Just as she was about to close the application in frustration, she stumbled upon Ashwin's profile.

Unlike the others, his profile didn't mention his salary bracket or his family's ancestral landholdings. Instead, his bio contained a simple paragraph about his unsuccessful attempt to cultivate a balcony garden, his collection of indie music cassettes, and his deep appreciation for a perfectly brewed cup of traditional filter coffee. Intrigued by the sudden flash of vulnerability in a sea of corporate armor, Meera tapped her finger against the glass screen. A soft chime echoed through the quiet room—a match had been made.
